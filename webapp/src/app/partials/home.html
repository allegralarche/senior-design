Counterfactual Coding

A senior design project at the university of Pennsylvania by Allegra Larche, Joey Raso, Kevin Zembroski, and Anthony Janocko

What Is Counterfactual Thinking?

Counterfactual thinking is a concept in psychology that involves the human tendency to create possible alternatives to life events that have already occurred

Why do we care?

Counterfactual thinking has been shown to be linked to certain aspects of one's personality such as aptitude for planning and affect management. Our project sought to apply this concept to a larger scale, instead studying if counterfactuals could be linked to greater socio-economic statistics. We used twitter as our platform for gathering data and correlated our findings against county-level statistics from the WWBP database. You can see our findings below.
